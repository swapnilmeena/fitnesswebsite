LEGS WORKOUT

  • Squats: A classic compound movement that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, lower your hips down as if sitting back into a chair, and then return to the starting position
  • Lunges: Another great compound exercise that works your quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Take a step forward or backward, lower your body until both knees are bent at 90 degrees, and then push through the front heel to return to the starting position
  • Deadlifts: While primarily targeting the hamstrings and glutes, deadlifts also engage the lower back and core. Bend at the hips while keeping your back straight, grab a barbell or weights, and stand up by extending your hips
  • Leg Press: Using a leg press machine, push the weight upward with your legs. This exercise primarily targets your quadriceps but also engages your hamstrings and glutes
  • Step-Ups: Use a bench or sturdy surface to step up and down. This exercise works your quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. You can hold weights for added resistance.
  • Calf Raises: Stand on the edge of a step or a platform, then raise and lower your heels to target the calf muscles.
  • Wall Sits: A static exercise that engages your quadriceps. Press your back against a wall and lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground, as if sitting in an invisible chair
  • Hamstring Curls: If you have access to a leg curl machine, this exercise isolates the hamstrings. Lie face down and curl your heels toward your glutes.
  • Bulgarian Split Squats: This variation of lunges involves elevating one foot behind you on a bench or platform while performing a lunge motion with the other leg. It targets the quadriceps and glutes.
