SHOULDER WORKOUT

  • Overhead Shoulder Press: Stand or sit with a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height. Press the dumbbells overhead until your arms are fully extended. Lower the dumbbells back down to shoulder heigh
  • Lateral Raises: Hold a dumbbell in each hand by your sides. Keeping your arms slightly bent, raise the dumbbells out to the sides until they are at shoulder level. Lower the dumbbells back down to your sides.
  • Front Raises: Hold a dumbbell in each hand in front of your thighs. Keeping your arms slightly bent, raise the dumbbells straight out in front of you until they are at shoulder level. Lower the dumbbells back down to your thighs.
  • Bent-Over Lateral Raises: Bend forward at the hips, keeping your back straight. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with your arms hanging down. Raise the dumbbells out to the sides until they are at shoulder level. Lower the dumbbells back down.
  • Face Pulls: Attach a rope handle to a cable machine at chest height. Grasp the rope with both hands and step back. Pull the rope towards your face, leading with your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together. Slowly release the rope back to the starting position.
  • Upright Rows: Hold a barbell or dumbbells in front of your thighs, hands about shoulder-width apart. Pull the weight straight up, leading with your elbows, until it reaches chest level. Lower the weight back down to your thighs.
  • External Rotations: Use a resistance band or a cable machine. Keep your elbow bent at a 90-degree angle and your upper arm close to your side. Rotate your forearm outward against the resistance
  • Prone Y Raises: Lie face down on an incline bench, holding light dumbbells in each hand. Raise your arms up and out to form a "Y" shape. Lower the dumbbells back down.
